<strong><strong>By Product</strong></h4>
The pharmaceutical industry is growing with the rise of oligonucleotide-based drugs, which use RNA/DNA to target disease genes/pathways, offering advantages over traditional small molecule drugs. Synthesized oligonucleotides, short single-stranded DNA/RNA molecules, have crucial applications in genetic testing, research and drug development. Made through chemical synthesis, the quality of synthesized oligonucleotides (accuracy and purity) determines their suitability for use in PCR, sequencing, and hybridization. The quality of reagents and equipment used in the synthesis process impacts the final product's accuracy, purity, and yield. Reagents include chemicals, enzymes, and materials, while high-quality equipment is in growing demand with the increase of oligonucleotide use in genetic testing and drug development.
<strong><strong>By Application</strong></h4>
The oligonucleotide synthesis market is segmented into three main applications: therapeutic, research, and diagnostic. Therapeutic applications of oligonucleotides include the use of antisense oligonucleotides, RNA interference, and aptamers for the treatment of various diseases such as cancer, genetic disorders, and cardiovascular diseases. Research applications of oligonucleotides include their use in gene expression analysis, genotyping, and epigenetic studies. Diagnostic applications of oligonucleotides include their use in disease diagnosis, pathogen detection, and personalized medicine.
