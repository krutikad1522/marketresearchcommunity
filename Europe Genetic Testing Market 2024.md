<strong><strong>By Type</strong></h4>
Type Segment is divided into Predictive &amp; Presymptomatic Testing, Carrier Testing, Diagnostic Testing, Newborn Testing. Predictive is type of testing which is used to identify individuals who have a high risk of developing a genetic disorder in the future. It's usually done when a person has a family history of a genetic disorder or when a genetic disorder is suspected based on other factors. Carrier testing are the type of testing used to identify individuals who carry genetic mutations that could potentially be passed on to their children. It's usually done by individuals who are planning to have a child or are pregnant. Newborn screening testing is done on newborns to detect certain genetic disorders or conditions that can be treated early on. While as prenatal testing is done to identify genetic disorders in unborn children. It's usually done during the first or second trimesters of pregnancy. Diagnostic testing helps to identify genetic disorders in individuals who have symptoms of a specific condition. Pharmacogenetic testing identifies genetic variations that may influence an individual's response to certain medications.
<strong><strong>By Technology</strong></h4>
PCR is a widely used technology that amplifies small segments of DNA. It's used to detect the presence of specific genetic mutations or variations. NGS is a newer technology that can analyze large amounts of genetic data quickly and accurately. It's used to identify genetic variations that may be related to the development of certain diseases or conditions. Hybridization-based methods are used to detect genetic variations by analyzing the binding of probes to specific regions of DNA. These are the main technologies used in genetic testing, each technology has its own advantages and disadvantages, and each technology is used for a specific purpose.
<strong><strong>By Application</strong></h4>
Cardiology testing is used in cardiology to identify genetic mutations that may be related to the development of heart disease. This helps to identify individuals who are at high risk of developing heart disease and inform treatment decisions. Treatment decisions, monitor treatment response, identify new treatment options, and identify recurrence and hereditary cancer. As genetic testing technologies continue to advance, the use of genetic testing in cancer diagnosis and management is expected to become increasingly widespread. Genetic testing can help to improve outcomes for cancer patients by providing more accurate and personalized treatment options.
<strong><strong>By Product</strong></h4>
The testing services include the analysis and interpretation of test results. They are provided by clinical laboratories, hospitals, or genetic testing companies. These services can include genetic counseling, which is essential for individuals who are considering genetic testing or who have received genetic test results. Software is used to analyze genetic test results and to interpret the data. It can be used to identify genetic variations to predict the risk of developing certain diseases or conditions, or to monitor the response of cancer cells to treatment.
<strong><strong>By End User</strong></h4>
Testing are often performed in hospitals and clinics. These facilities typically provide genetic testing services to patients who have been referred by a healthcare provider. Clinical laboratories use various genetic testing technologies, such as PCR, NGS, and microarray, to perform a wide range of genetic tests.
