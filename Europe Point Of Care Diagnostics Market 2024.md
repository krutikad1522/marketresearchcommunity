<strong><strong>By Purchase</strong></h4>
The point-of-care diagnostic market can be segmented by purchase into two categories: OTC Testing Products and Prescription-based Testing Products. OTC Testing Products refer to over-the-counter tests that can be purchased without a prescription, such as pregnancy tests and glucose monitoring devices. Prescription-based Testing Products refer to tests that require a prescription from a healthcare provider, such as infectious disease testing products and cancer marker testing products.
<strong><strong>By Platform</strong></h4>
The POC diagnostic market is segmented by platform into Lateral Flow Assays, Immunoassays, Microfluidics, Molecular Diagnostics, Dipsticks. Lateral Flow uses paper-based tests for quick, simple, cost-effective diagnoses. Immunoassays detect antigens with antibodies, widely used in POC testing. Microfluidics manipulates small fluid volumes in POC diagnostic devices. Molecular Diagnostics detects DNA/RNA sequences, growing in POC testing. Dipsticks use paper strips to detect substances in samples, simple diagnostic tests.
